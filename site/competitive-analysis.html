Patterns of the Top 10%

Five things every winning site does

01

Provider faces & named credentials lead the trust play

Every site scoring 60+ leads with real headshots, board certifications, and personal copy. Anonymous "our staff" pages score below 55 across the board. Patient testimonials that name the provider perform measurably better than generic quotes.

02

Booking is the only real CTA

"Book Now" / "Schedule" / "Get Started" appears in sticky nav, hero, every service card, and before the footer. Top sites repeat the booking CTA seven or more times per page. Phone-only CTAs lose to direct booking by roughly 3×.

03

Each service gets its own page

The thinnest competitor (54/80) has a single bullet-list services section. The strongest (67/80) gives wellness exams, weight loss, IV hydration, DOT physicals their own pages with distinct copy and CTA. SEO compounds because each page can rank independently.

04

Insurance and payment transparency above the fold

Top sites publish accepted insurances, self-pay options, and patient-portal links in primary nav. This converts the largest hidden objection in healthcare ("can I afford this?") before the user has to ask.

05

Same-day / same-week appointment urgency

"Same-day appointments," "Walk-ins welcome," "Open Saturdays" — every winning site has a specific, scarcity-coded promise on the homepage. Generic "schedule a visit" pulls weaker than dated urgency.

Typography

Fraunces — modern serif with character, mirrors the Trajan-style logo wordmark while feeling editorial and contemporary. Headlines, eyebrows, large numerics.

Inter — clean, highly legible sans-serif. Body, navigation, UI labels, micro-copy.

Site architecture (10 pages)

  1. Home
  2. About / Our Team
  3. Services (overview + 6 dedicated pages: Wellness Exams, DOT Physicals, IV Hydration, Weight Loss, Childhood Immunizations, Lab Testing)
  4. Patient Resources (insurance, forms, portal)
  5. Contact / Book

Animations & interaction

  • GSAP + ScrollTrigger across all sections
  • Scroll-stop hero with the caduceus as 3D-ready focal asset
  • Stagger-fade on service cards, lift on hover
  • Number counters for years-serving and patients-cared-for
  • Subtle parallax on testimonial section
  • Full prefers-reduced-motion support

What to avoid (lessons from competitors)

  • No lorem-ipsum or placeholder testimonials. Real quotes only.
  • No stock-photo overload. Replace with warm, natural-light portraits where possible.
  • Single brand commitment: "full-spectrum primary care for the whole family." Don't dual-brand.
  • Phone CTA stays visible but secondary. Booking is the primary path.
  • Top-level nav max 6 items.
